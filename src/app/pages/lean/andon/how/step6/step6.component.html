6. Train Personnel

Educate employees on the Andon system to ensure effective utilization and response. Comprehensive training is crucial for the success of the Andon system:

Operation: Train employees on how to trigger an alert, interpret indicators, and respond appropriately. This includes:
  • Understanding the different types of alerts and their meanings.
  • Learning how to manually trigger an alert using buttons or cords.
  • Recognizing automated alerts generated by sensors and systems.
  • Knowing the immediate steps to take when an alert is triggered, such as stopping the production line or calling for assistance.
Proper training ensures that employees can quickly and accurately respond to alerts, minimizing downtime and maintaining safety.
Responsibilities: Define clear roles and responsibilities for different levels of alerts. This includes:
  • Identifying who is responsible for responding to specific types of alerts.
  • Ensuring supervisors and managers are aware of their roles in investigating and resolving issues.
  • Establishing protocols for escalating alerts to higher levels of management if necessary.
  • Providing ongoing training and refreshers to keep everyone up-to-date with procedures and best practices.
Clearly defined responsibilities ensure that there is no confusion during an alert, and that issues are resolved efficiently.

Regular training sessions and drills can help reinforce knowledge and ensure that all personnel are prepared to effectively use the Andon system. Continuous education and practice are key to maintaining a responsive and efficient operational environment.
