Step-by-Step Guide to Setting Up Andon Visual Management

1. Define the Purpose and Scope

Determine what processes or areas will benefit from Andon visual management. Identify key metrics or parameters you want to monitor.

2. Select Visual Indicators

Choose visual indicators (lights, boards, displays) to represent the status of operations:

  • Lights: Green for normal, yellow for attention required, red for stoppage.
  • Boards: Physical or digital displays showing real-time data.

3. Design Andon Board Layout

Create the layout for your Andon board:

  • Sections: Divide into areas if monitoring multiple processes.
  • Labels: Clearly label each section or indicator.
  • Information Display: Decide what to display (e.g., process status, time since last incident).

4. Choose Andon Trigger Mechanisms

Determine what events or conditions will trigger an Andon alert:

  • Manual: Operators press a button or pull a cord to indicate an issue.
  • Automated: Sensors detect abnormalities in production metrics (e.g., downtime, error rates).

5. Implement Visual Controls

Install and integrate the chosen visual indicators with your workspace:

  • Lights: Mount Andon lights where they are easily visible to all relevant personnel.
  • Boards: Set up digital displays or physical boards in strategic locations.

6. Train Personnel

Educate employees on the Andon system:

  • Operation: How to trigger an alert, interpret indicators, and respond appropriately.
  • Responsibilities: Define roles and responsibilities for different levels of alerts.

7. Establish Response Procedures

Develop clear protocols for responding to Andon alerts:

  • Escalation: Define who to notify for each level of alert (e.g., team leader, maintenance).
  • Resolution: Standard procedures for addressing and resolving issues promptly.

8. Monitor and Improve

Continuously monitor the effectiveness of your Andon system:

  • Feedback: Solicit feedback from employees on system usability and effectiveness.
  • Metrics: Track key performance indicators (KPIs) related to response times and issue resolution.

9. Iterate and Optimize

Based on feedback and data, make iterative improvements to your Andon system:

  • Adjustments: Modify triggers or indicators as processes evolve.
  • Training: Provide ongoing training to ensure all personnel are proficient with the system.

10. Integrate with Continuous Improvement

Incorporate Andon insights into your continuous improvement processes:

  • Root Cause Analysis: Use Andon data to identify recurring issues and implement permanent solutions.
  • Kaizen: Encourage a culture of continuous improvement based on Andon system feedback.

Example Tools and Technologies

  • Hardware: Andon lights, digital displays, push buttons.
  • Software: Andon board software for digital displays.
  • Integration: Connect Andon triggers with production monitoring systems.
